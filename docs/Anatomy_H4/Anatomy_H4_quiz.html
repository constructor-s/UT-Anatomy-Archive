Question 1

1. From the options listed below, choose the CORRECT statement(s) regarding the neuromuscular junction and excitation-contraction coupling in skeletal muscle.

Choices:

  • In order for cross bridge binding to take place, the active sites on actin filaments must be uncovered
  • The binding of acetylcholine to receptors on the surface of a muscle fibre initiates an electrical signal in the sarcolemma
  • Acetylcholine is the neurotransmitter at skeletal muscle neuromuscular junctions
  • At a neuromuscular junction, the synaptic terminal of a neuron communicates with the motor end plate of a skeletal muscle fibre
  • An electrical signal along the transverse tubules causes the sarcoplasmic reticulum to actively take up Ca2+
  • Acetylcholinesterase is an enzyme that is responsible for the production of acetylcholine in the synaptic terminal

Answers:

  • In order for cross bridge binding to take place, the active sites on actin filaments must be uncovered
  • The binding of acetylcholine to receptors on the surface of a muscle fibre initiates an electrical signal in the sarcolemma
  • Acetylcholine is the neurotransmitter at skeletal muscle neuromuscular junctions
  • At a neuromuscular junction, the synaptic terminal of a neuron communicates with the motor end plate of a skeletal muscle fibre

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! NMJ refers to the point of communication between a neuron and a skeletal muscle fibres. Signal propagation requires ACh to bind to AChRs at the motor endplate. Ca2+ binding to troponin uncovers the active site on actin filaments for cross bridge binding.

Question 2

2. From the options listed below, choose the CORRECT statement(s) regarding motor units in skeletal muscle.

Choices:

  • All muscle fibres in a given motor unit contract at the same time
  • Muscles that must be precisely controlled have smaller motor units
  • The force generated by a muscle is proportional to the number of motor units recruited
  • Larger motor units contain more motor neurons
  • Multiple motor neurons synapse onto each muscle fibre

Answers:

  • All muscle fibres in a given motor unit contract at the same time
  • Muscles that must be precisely controlled have smaller motor units
  • The force generated by a muscle is proportional to the number of motor units recruited

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! A motor unit is a single motor neuron and all the muscle fibres it innervates. It contracts as a whole, and the force developed is proportional to the number of motor units activated. Small motor units, allow more precise control of a muscle.
