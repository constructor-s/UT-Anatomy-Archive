20A Quiz

Question 1

1. Regarding the functional subdivisions of the cerebellum, the flocculus and nodule comprise the ____________________.

Choices:

  • vestibulocerebellum

Answers:

  • vestibulocerebellum

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! The flocculus and nodule comprise the vestibulocerebellum, a functional subdivision of the cerebellum that concerns itself with posture and balance.

Question 2

2. Information regarding movements that are planned or ongoing enters the cerebellum via the _________________ cerebellar peduncle.

Choices:

  • middle

Answers:

  • middle

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! The cerebellum receives information about anticipated movement through pontocerebellar input. The pontocerebellar fibres make up the middle cerebellar peduncle.

Question 3

3. Disorders of gait and balance can occur with damage to the _____________ cerebellar peduncle.

Choices:

  • inferior

Answers:

  • inferior

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! The inferior cerebellar peduncle influences balance and gait through the ipsilateral vestibulospinal and reticulospinal pathways respectively.

20B Quiz

Question 1

1. Most brain capillaries are of the ______________ type.

Choices:

  • continuous

Answers:

  • continuous

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! Most brain capillaries are continuous, meaning that the endothelial cells are joined by tight junctions (TJs). TJs limit molecular movement via the paracellular route.
  • Continue

Question 2

2. This image demonstrates an example of a(n) ______________ hydrocephalus.

Choices:

  • obstructive

Answers:

  • obstructive

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! The image demonstrates an example of an obstructive hydrocephalus. Notice the enlargement of the lateral and third ventricles but not the fourth.

Question 4

4. Fluid is free to move, unimpeded, across the ependymal lining of the choroid plexus.

Choices:

  • True
  • False

Answers:

  • False

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! The choroid ependymal cells are joined by TJs, therefore, fluid is unable to move freely across ependymal lining of choroid plexus.

Choices:

  • tumour
  • inflammation
  • arteriovenous malformations
  • hydrocephalus
  • acute stroke

Answers:

  • tumour
  • inflammation
  • arteriovenous malformations

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! BBB breakdown occurs in tumour, inflammation and arteriovenous malformation. BBB breakdown occurs in later stages of stroke, but not in the acute phase.
