Answers:

Question 4

4. A ____________ ______________ is an intracranial accumulation of blood that is characterized by gyral enhancement as seen in brain imaging.

Choices:

  • subarachnoid hemorrhage

Answers:

  • subarachnoid hemorrhage

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! Rupture of cerebral arteries spills blood into the subarachnoid space, including the sulci between the gyri. The high signal blood in these spaces illuminate the gyri. This is gyral enhancement.

  • epidural hematoma

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! Meningeal arteries lie between the periosteal layer of the dura and the inner table of the cranial bones. When these vessels rupture, the periosteum is torn from the bony surfaces, causing an epidural hematoma.

Question 12

12. An ____________ ______________ is an intracranial accumulation of blood that typically appears lens-shaped in brain imaging.

Choices:

  • extradural hematoma
  • epidural hematoma

Answers:

  • extradural hematoma
  • epidural hematoma

Comments:

  • Correct
  • That’s right! The spread of blood in an epidural hematoma is limited by sutures where the periosteum is continuous between the internal and external surfaces of the cranial bones. This explains the typical lens shape of this type of intracranial bleed.

  • That’s right! There is no anatomical structure to limit the spread of blood in the (normally potential) space between the arachnoid and dura, so the shape of a subdural hematoma mirrors that of the internal surface of the cranium.
