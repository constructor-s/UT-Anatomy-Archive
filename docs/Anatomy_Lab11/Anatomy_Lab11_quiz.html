Answers:

Question 2

2. A duodenal ulcer erodes the gut wall, but does not perforate it. Which of the following describes the resulting pain? Choose all that apply.

Choices:

  • The pain will be felt in the epigastric region.
  • the patient might describe the pain as dull and achy
  • the patient will be vague in describing the location of the pain
  • The pain will be felt in the pubic (hypogastric) region.
  • The pain will be felt in the umbilical region.
  • the patient might describe the pain as sharp and severe.
  • the patient will be very precise in describing the location of the pain

  • The pain will be felt in the epigastric region.
  • the patient might describe the pain as dull and achy
  • the patient will be vague in describing the location of the pain

  • Correct
  • That's right! Ulcer pain might also be described as “burning” pain, instead of dull and achy.
